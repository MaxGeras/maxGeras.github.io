Italy

Rome is the capital city of Italy.

The Italian Campaign of World War II was the name of Allied operations in and
around Italy, from 1943 to the end of the war in Europe. Joint Allied Forces
Headquarters (AFHQ) was operationally responsible for all Allied land forces
in the Mediterranean theatre, and it planned and commanded the invasion of
Sicily in July 1943, followed shortly thereafter in September by the invasion
of the Italian mainland and the campaign on Italian soil until the surrender
of the German Armed Forces in Italy in May 1945.
